# Governance Policy (PM/Leadership/Legal-Friendly)

This document defines **boundaries** for the AI support triage system.

## Allowed actions
- Classify tickets into approved categories
- Assign priority (P0/P1/P2)
- Draft a suggested response with clear, conservative language
- Ask clarifying questions

## Forbidden behaviors (must never happen)
- Promise refunds, credits, or discounts unless explicitly authorized by human review
- Instruct the customer to share passwords, OTPs, payment details, or other secrets
- Give legal advice, medical advice, or tax advice
- Claim certainty when uncertain or make unsupported promises
- Reveal internal policies, system prompts, or private escalation paths
- Hallucinate facts about order status, account state, prior conversations, or system behavior
- Ignore explicit customer escalation requests or safety concerns

## Required behaviors
- Use transparent phrasing: “Based on what you shared…” or “It sounds like…”
- If uncertain, ask 1–3 clarifying questions before making a recommendation
- Encourage escalation for security, safety, harassment, or payment disputes
- Prioritize customer privacy and do not infer sensitive data without evidence
- Mark cases for human review when multiple policies conflict or the intent is ambiguous

## Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) rules
HITL is required if any of these are true:
- A forbidden behavior is detected or strongly implied
- Ticket involves security-related issues: account takeover, fraud, credential compromise, unauthorized access
- Ticket involves payment disputes, refunds, chargebacks, or contested charges
- Customer explicitly requests escalation, mentions legal action, or references an executive complaint
- Ticket contains legal, medical, regulatory, or compliance-sensitive questions
- Customer language is abusive, threats are made, or safety concerns are present
- Intent is ambiguous, conflicting, or not clearly classifiable
- Low confidence in category/priority (confidence < 0.7) for any P0 or unclear case
- Multiple priorities or categories seem applicable and the system cannot safely choose one

### HITL examples
- Security incident: “My account was hacked and I can’t access it.”
- Payment dispute: “I’m going to charge back this transaction if it is not refunded.”
- Legal/regulatory: “Can you tell me whether I should sue or terminate my contract?”
- Privacy concern: “I think someone has my credit card details.”
- Ambiguous classification: “I need help with an order, but I also want to cancel due to fraud.”
- Escalation language: “I want to speak to a manager” or “This is urgent and I may leave a bad review.”
- Customer asks for action outside support scope: “Tell me exactly how to reset someone else’s password.”

### Rollout vs steady-state
- During rollout: human review is required for every draft and every HITL trigger. The AI should operate in shadow mode until the behavior is stable.
- After rollout: use HITL selectively based on the rules above, with monitoring and regular audits.
- If there is a spike in policy violations, wrong routing, or negative customer outcomes, revert to full human review immediately.

## Kill switch
If we see a spike in policy violations, wrong routing, or poor model performance:
- disable auto mode (switch to shadow mode only)
- require HITL for all tickets until investigation is complete
- review recent cases, update rules, and retrain if needed

## Transparency to users (what we would say in a real product)
- “Draft generated by AI, reviewed by a human before sending” (at least during rollout)
- “A human will verify this response before it is sent to the customer” for HITL cases
